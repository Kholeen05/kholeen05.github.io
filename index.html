Location

Cafe Ilang-Ilang

• Food : Luxury

Café Ilang-Ilang is a luxurious restaurant located in the historic Manila Hotel. It’s known for an extensive buffet selection that includes Japanese cuisine, Italian, Chinese, western, Asian, Filipino, and more. Their live cooking stations ensure that the food is well presented and of the highest quality. Sample their salad station, cold cuts and seafood station, burrito and shawarma station, and bread and cheese station.

Barbara's Heritage Restaurant

• Food

Barbara’s Heritage Restaurant serves genuine Filipino cuisine with a real Filipino ambience. Located on General Luna Street, resident musicians play serenades during lunchtime, before being joined by dancers in the evening, offering guests a very pleasant show while they enjoy their dinner. If you come to Manila and want to have an authentic Filipino experience, then Barbara’s should be your place of choice.

Sky Deck View Bar

• Food : Nightlife

Sky Deck View Bar is a stylish lounge and restaurant on the rooftop of The Bayleaf Intramuros. The venue is a stunning sundowner spot, offering views over Manila Bay. Food-wise, you can enjoy both Filipino and international cuisine, served in big portions at fair rates. A live music band is in charge of the entertainment on some nights, but the highlight of the Sky Deck remains the sunset over Manila.

Dreamland Café

• Drink : Relax

This cafe is ideal for artistic people or those who want to relax in an inspiring space. This bohemian-inspired joint is part restaurant and part creative outlet. This famous coffee shop in the Philippines serves hot and cold drinks, including barakong Batangueño and tsokolateng Batangueña. For food, they have appetizers, rice meals, and pasta.

St. Marc Café

• Drink : Relax

St. Marc Café. The cafe combines delectable desserts with premium coffee in a casual ambiance. The pilot store opened in Singapore in 2012, and in 2014, entrepreneur Ben Chan brought the franchise to the country. This famous coffee shop in the Philippines has 16 outlets in Southeast Asia, including one in Metro Manila, Philippines. While they may be known for their choco cro, they also have a sizable drinks menu with their sweet treats. One of these is the quintessentially Japanese matcha latte. Vietnamese coffee and cappuccino have also been proven hits with their customers.
